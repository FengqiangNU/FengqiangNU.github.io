Fengqiang Li

I am currently a fifth year computer science Ph.D. student working with Oliver Cossairt in Computational Photography Lab at Northwestern University. My research is in computational photography and computer vision. I received my M.S in Electrical Engineering from Lehigh University in 2014, where I worked on optical coherence tomography (OCT) with Chao Zhou. Before that, I got my B.S in Optics from Huazhong University of Science and Technology.

In the past summers, I was fortunated to work with Michael Hall and Shuochen Su on stereo for AR devices at Facebook AR/VR (2019 summer), with Michael Hall on modeling and evaluation of novel ToF cameras at Oculus VR (2018 summer), and with Emily Cooper on Robotics Vision at Google X (2016 summer).

Research

I am working on computational algorithms to improve the quality of 3D imaging using optimization and deep learning. I am interested in 3D imaging from micro-scale OCT to marco scale time-of-flight imaging, Photo Stereo, and photometric stereo. I am also interested in 3D applications powered with state-of-the-art machine/deep learning for high level computer vision problems such as face recognition and SLAM, and machine perceptions in self-driving cars, robots and VR/AR systems.
